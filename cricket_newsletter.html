Cricket Daily Digest

December 17, 2024

Latest News

Calf strain set to force Hazlewood out of the remainder of India series

Josh Hazlewood is unlikely to feature in the remainder of the Test series against India after suffering a calf strain on the fourth day of the Brisbane Test. Scans confirmed a right calf strain, forcing him to miss the Test. Hazlewood was already recovering from side injuries, which kept him out of several Tests. Scott Boland is likely to be recalled for the Boxing Day Test in Melbourne.

'We are definitely concerned' - BCB chief selector on Litton's form

Bangladesh chief selector Gazi Ashraf Hossain expressed concern about Litton Kumar Das' white-ball cricket form, citing him out of the playing XI for a decisive match. Ashraf suggested a break or adjustments could be arranged if needed. They are also uncertain about the participation of Tamim Iqbal and Shakib Al Hasan in the upcoming Champions Trophy due to various issues, including Shakib's politics-related issues and Tamim's need to improve his fitness.

Sammy appointed West Indies' all-format coach

Cricket West Indies announced Daren Sammy as the head coach for all senior men's teams, effective April 1, 2025. Sammy, a former team captain, has been the white-ball head coach since May 2023, leading West Indies to several series wins. Andre Coley will no longer lead the Test team. A new CEO, Chris Dehring, is set to join from February 1, 2025, replacing Johnny Grave who stepped down in October.

Ankit Rajpoot retires from Indian cricket at 31

Uttar Pradesh pacer Ankit Rajpoot, aged 31, has announced his retirement from Indian cricket. The lanky bowler holds 248 wickets in first-class cricket and 24 IPL wickets, with a five-wicket haul. Having represented multiple IPL franchises, Rajpoot will now explore new cricket opportunities and the business side, stating it as the next step in his journey.

Southee signs off on a happy note with a "pretty special" win

Tim Southee wrapped up his 17-year cricket career on a high note with a 423-run win against England, as hosts New Zealand gave England a hammering in the final Test. Southee ends his career with 776 international wickets and an incredible journey of satisfying memories, praised the next generation of bowlers and expressed joy in retiring with a World Test Championship triumph and a strong bond with his teammates.

He needs to get his work ethics right - Shreyas Iyer on Prithvi Shaw

Prithvi Shaw, a talented young cricketer, continues to struggle with his performance. Despite flashes of brilliance, he has faced setbacks like being left out of the Mumbai squad in October 2024 and going unsold in the IPL 2025 auction. Former coaches Ricky Ponting and Pravin Amre have expressed concerns over his indiscipline and fitness, highlighting the need for Shaw to improve his work ethics to reach his potential.

We are in transition so it's my job to help the others - Bumrah

India's pace spearhead Jasprit Bumrah defended his team's bowling unit, which has struggled in the series against Australia. Despite his standout performance, Bumrah emphasized that every player has a role to play. He attributed his success to adapting to different conditions, not expecting extra pressure, and focusing on personal growth. Bumrah also backed his teammate Mohammed Siraj, who is dealing with a niggle. With two days of play remaining, India faces a tough challenge in the fourth Test.

At that stage I was thinking about going and padding up - Rahul

KL Rahul praised the efforts of Jasprit Bumrah and Akash Deep as India reduced the deficit to 193 runs heading into the final day's play in Brisbane. Bumrah and Deep added an unbeaten 39-run stand, avoiding the follow-on. Rahul acknowledged the lower-order batsmen's efforts, stating that they had been working on batting hard in the nets.

Shaw left out of Mumbai squad for start of Vijay Hazare Trophy

Mumbai has named its squad for the first three matches of the Vijay Hazare Trophy, leaving out Prithvi Shaw. Shaw reacted to the exclusion with an Instagram post expressing his disappointment, but Mumbai captain Rohit Sharma said Shaw needs to improve his work ethics to achieve his full potential and that it's up to him to figure out his game. Ayush Mhatre, an uncapped 17-year-old, has been included in the squad.

Dercksen the latest victim of CSA's DRS disaster

A disastrous day of cricket in Bloemfontein occurred when Annerie Dercksen was given out caught despite clear evidence of not hitting the ball. CSA's decision not to use DRS despite its availability for other international matches likely added to the controversy. Laura Wolvaardt was reprimanded for dissent over an lbw dismissal, but her frustration was justified, while umpire Kerrin Klaaste was among those laying charges against her, sparking accusations of tone-deafness.
